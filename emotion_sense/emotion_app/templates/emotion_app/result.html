Valence-Arousal (V-A) Space
Valence-Arousal (V-A) Space
  • Valence: Indicates how pleasant or unpleasant an emotion is. It ranges from negative (unpleasant) to positive (pleasant).
  • Arousal: Indicates the intensity of the emotion, ranging from low (calm) to high (excited).
The Four Quadrants (4Q)

Based on the combination of valence and arousal values, emotions are divided into four quadrants:

Quadrant 1 (Q1): High Valence, High Arousal
  • Emotions: Happy, Excited, Joyful
  • Characteristics: Pleasant and highly energetic
Quadrant 2 (Q2): Low Valence, High Arousal
  • Emotions: Angry, Anxious, Tense
  • Characteristics: Unpleasant and highly energetic
Quadrant 3 (Q3): Low Valence, Low Arousal
  • Emotions: Sad, Depressed, Bored
  • Characteristics: Unpleasant and low energy
Quadrant 4 (Q4): High Valence, Low Arousal
  • Emotions: Calm, Relaxed, Content
  • Characteristics: Pleasant and low energy
